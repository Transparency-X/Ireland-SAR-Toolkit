# FOI vs SAR: Strategic Comparison

## Freedom of Information (FOI) Act 2014
- **Purpose**: Access to records held by public bodies.
- **Scope**: Public bodies only (government departments, local authorities, HSE, etc.).
- **Exemptions**: Cabinet records, commercially sensitive information, deliberative processes.
- **Fees**: €15 application fee (waived for personal information requests).
- **Response Time**: 20 working days (extendable by 20 working days).
- **Right to**: Access to **records**, not limited to personal data.

## Subject Access Request (SAR)
- **Purpose**: Access to **your personal data** held by any organisation.
- **Scope**: All controllers (public and private).
- **Exemptions**: Limited (third-party data, legal privilege).
- **Fees**: Free (unless manifestly unfounded or repetitive).
- **Response Time**: 1 calendar month (extendable by 2 months for complex requests).
- **Right to**: Personal data **plus** purposes, categories, recipients, retention periods, automated decision-making.

## When to Use Which?
| Scenario | FOI | SAR |
|---|---|---|
| Your own personal data from public body | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes (preferred: faster, free, broader info) |
| Non-personal records from public body | ✅ Yes | ❌ No |
| Private company | ❌ No | ✅ Yes |
| Urgent request | ❌ 20 working days | ✅ 1 month |

## Strategic Advice
If a department resists a broad SAR as "excessive," consider:
1. Narrowing the SAR to specific data categories.
2. Filing a separate **FOI request** for administrative context.
3. The two regimes are **complementary**, not mutually exclusive.
